📋 Reference Clauses — Schedule A1
A1-1:1 Deposit Delivery (Certified Cheque/Bank Draft)
The Buyer hereby acknowledges and agrees to deliver the Deposit as mentioned in this Agreement of Purchase and Sale by Bank Draft or Certified cheque to the Deposit Holder within 24 hours of the acceptance of this Agreement.
A1-1:2 Deposit Timeline & Default Remedy
Buyer agrees to deliver the above-mentioned Deposit to the Listing Brokerage's office within 24 hours of the time stated in the "Confirmation of Acceptance" section of this Agreement of Purchase and Sale. TIME IS OF THE ESSENCE with respect to this obligation. In the event that the Listing Brokerage does not receive the said Deposit within this period, the Seller may, at their sole and absolute discretion, declare this Agreement terminated due to Buyer's default. Upon such termination, the Seller shall be at liberty to immediately re-list the property for sale and to accept another offer, and the Seller reserves all rights to pursue any and all legal remedies against the Buyer, including, but not limited to, a claim for damages.
A1-1:3 No Warranty on Chattels
The Seller(s) and Buyer acknowledge and agree that there is no express or implied warranty on the condition of the chattels which are included in this Agreement of Purchase and Sale.
A1-1+2:4 Seller's Right to Adjust Completion Date
All parties acknowledge and agree that notwithstanding the completion date set out in this offer, the Seller(s) has the right to advance or postpone the completion date of the transaction by not more than 45 days once, by giving written notice of the amended completion date to the Buyer or his solicitor at least 30 days in advance of the earlier of the completion date set out therein and the amended completion date. All parties also acknowledge and agree that the Title Search Date will be changed to 10 business days before the amended completion date accordingly.
🛡 Seller Communication Strategies ▼
Transparent Communication
  • Present every offer promptly with written net proceeds summary, not just headline price
  • Explain recommended counter-strategy before acting
  • Never let seller feel surprised — they know what's coming before it happens
Data-Driven Advice
  • Pull comparable sales to anchor seller's expectations and justify counter position
  • Show days-on-market trends to contextualize urgency (or lack thereof)
  • Present net sheet with every scenario so they see financial reality clearly
Pace Control
  • Don't rush back to buyer — measured response signals confidence and strength
  • Use irrevocable expiry times strategically to create urgency without desperation
  • If multiple offers: manage process so seller feels empowered, not pressured
Probe Buyer's Position
  • Ask buyer's agent: financing status, closing flexibility, competing properties
  • Use intelligence to advise seller on how hard to push and where to hold firm
Clear Recommendations
  • Give firm recommendation, not just options — sellers hire you for judgment
  • Explain the risk of each path (e.g. "if we counter at X, they may walk")
  • Document advice in writing so seller sees diligence
Emotional Management
  • Acknowledge emotional weight, then redirect to strategy and numbers
  • Your composure itself signals you have control of the situation
Highlight What You Fought For
  • After sign-back, explicitly tell seller what you pushed back on and why
  • Sellers don't realize what happened behind the scenes unless you tell them
Core Principle: Sellers feel protected when they feel informed, consulted, and never surprised. Every move must feel deliberate and in their interest — not reactive.
🤝 Negotiation Playbook ▼
1 · Preparation & Research
  • Market analysis: current conditions, recent comps, inventory levels
  • Property valuation: CMA, appraisal, unique features considered
  • Buyer profile research: motivations, financial situation, timeline
  • Financial preparedness: bottom line, desired price, all costs mapped
  • Contingency planning: anticipate inspection findings, financing delays
  • Documentation gathered: deeds, surveys, disclosures, repair records
2 · Objectives & Strategy
  • Goals defined: highest price, quickest sale, specific closing date, minimize repairs
  • Priorities ranked: non-negotiables vs. flexible items
  • Strategy outlined: initial ask, counter-offer ranges, limits
  • Strengths and weaknesses of property identified
  • Walk-away point determined
3 · Communication & Presentation
  • Clear, concise, professional communication — verbal and written
  • Highlight property value and unique selling points effectively
  • Active listening: understand buyer's needs, concerns, perspective
  • Maintain professionalism and composure throughout
  • Timely responses to maintain momentum
  • Use agent as liaison and buffer
4 · Concession & Compromise
  • Identify areas for flexibility: price, closing date, minor repairs, appliances
  • Assess perceived value of concessions from buyer's perspective
  • Plan trade-offs: what to gain in exchange (e.g. higher price for quicker close)
  • Evaluate and prioritize buyer's demands by criticality
  • Aim for "win-win" — both parties feel they achieved favorable outcome
5 · Handling Objections
  • Anticipated potential buyer concerns (price, condition, market trends)
  • Prepared factual, well-reasoned responses to common objections
  • Address concerns professionally without becoming defensive
  • Open to creative solutions to overcome obstacles
  • Leverage market data or property features to counter objections
  • Emotional intelligence: manage emotions (yours and the buyer's)
6 · Closing the Deal
  • Draft and review all agreements carefully with agent/lawyer
  • Meet all stipulated deadlines: inspections, financing, documents
  • Address and fulfill all conditions/contingencies
  • All agreed terms accurately reflected in final contract
  • Post-negotiation follow-up: maintain communication until closing
